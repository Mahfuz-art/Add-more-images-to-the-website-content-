Bandarban

Bandarban is a scenic district in southeastern Bangladesh, known for its lush green hills, dense forests, and rich tribal culture. Located in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, it's a popular destination for nature lovers and adventurers. Key attractions include Nilgiri, the highest accessible peak in Bangladesh, Boga Lake, and the serene Nafakhum waterfall. Bandarban is home to various indigenous communities, such as the Marma, Chakma, and Tripura, who add to its vibrant cultural diversity. The district offers trekking, river cruises, and an escape into unspoiled nature.

Saint Martin's Island is a small coral island located in the northeastern part of the Bay of Bengal, off the coast of Bangladesh. It's the country's only coral island, known for its crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches, and vibrant marine life. Popularly called "Narikel Jinjira" due to its abundance of coconut trees, the island is a serene getaway for tourists seeking tranquility and natural beauty. Visitors enjoy activities like snorkeling, exploring coral reefs, and watching stunning sunsets. The island's peaceful environment and simple lifestyle make it a unique destination in Bangladesh.
